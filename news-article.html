Launching Oretshotse

OCTOBER 2018

Oretshotse Holdings is a new company with old roots. It brings together companies that have a proud heritage of servicing the mining industry, especially in the Rustenburg platinum belt.

Companies such as Prino Contract Mining which will now be branded Oretshotse Contract Mining and Sachii that will be rebranded Oretshotse Engineering.

GRE Oretshotse will evolve into Oretshotse Manufacturing and Supply.
Also, in the group will be Oretshotse Open Cast Mining and Oretshotse Mining Services.
And opportunities outside the mining industry will also be explored under the banner of Oretshotse
Investments.

Peter Molotsi, CEO of Oretshotse Holdings, rationalises this as follows:
“Given the challenges facing mining in general and the Rustenburg platinum belt in particular, Oretshotse needs its brand to be a badge of trust that demands market share and creates barriers to entry; it must also facilitate spreading risk and finding opportunity by extending into other areas of activity and geography. To do this the brand needs to promote a common understanding internally and externally that attracts talent by providing purpose, direction and motivation.”

This recognises the firm belief that Oretshotse has in people, its own and those that it is privileged to serve. As David Ngubane, Director of Mining and New Business Development at Oretshotse Holdings, puts it:

“We believe in the beneficiation of people by which we mean giving them the opportunity to be their best. Because we believe in our people and respect those we serve, we can make the promise that we specialise in cutting through constraints to consistently perform efficiently.”

Martin Stander, Operations Manager for Oretshotse Open Cast Mining, endorses this belief in people, pointing out that the company is rich in experience – he himself has 40 years’ experience in mining: “Our vision is to measure our success in terms of the number of people we benefit: our own people, energised by opportunity; those we are privileged to serve; and the communities that are the source of both our inspiration and our endeavour.”
